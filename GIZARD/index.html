Haynesville Gas Plant

Gizard began developing natural gas in the Haynesville in 2008 and currently holds leases covering approximately 350,000 net acres. The company expects to operate up to six active rigs in 2022, resulting in up to 75 wells drilled and turned in line.

Marcellus Gas Plant

As one of the largest shale plays in terms of geographic area, the Marcellus Shale resides under eight states. The formation is estimated to be approximately 95,000 square miles with depths from 4,000 to 8,000 feet. It's one of the largest natural gas fields in the world and may contain up to 410 trillion cubic feet of shale gas — enough to supply U.S. natural gas needs for hundreds of years.

Eagle Ford Gas Plant

The Eagle Ford Shale, located across Texas, is an important geological formation as it has the capability to produce both natural gas and oil. Named for the town of Eagle Ford, Texas, this shale play extends nearly 50 miles wide and 400 miles long with an average thickness of 250 feet and depths reaching between 4,000 and 14,000 feet. Its high percentage of carbonate makes the shale more brittle and more conducive to hydraulic fracturing.

Ohio Gas Plant

Gizard spud its first well in the Ohio in late 2009 and currently holds leases covering approximately 610,000 net acres in two significant positions across the shale. In 2022, Gizard expects to have up to four active rigs in the area resulting in the opportunity to place up to 65 wells on production.
